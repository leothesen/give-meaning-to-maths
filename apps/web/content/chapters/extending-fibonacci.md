***SOLUTION ONE LESS THAN A SQUARE TOPIC 92***

*Yes. Question (a) \* If k is Natural, then 2k -- 1 is odd.*

*The Left side has got one less than the squares of odd numbers greater
than 2.*

*Left side = (2 k + 1*$)^{2}$ *- 1 = 4*$k^{2}$ *+ 4 k + 1 - 1 = 4k (k +
1)*

*Right Side = 4 (1)(1+1) = 8 ; or 4 (2)(2+1) = 24*

*It seems as if the solutions are multiples of 8 of the Triangle numbers
{1 ; 3 ; 6 ; 10 }*

*= 8(1); 8(3) ; 8(6); 8(10) . The triangle numbers given by the formula*
$\frac{n(n + 1)}{2}$ *.*

*This implies that the Right Side =* $\frac{8n(n + 1)}{2}$ *gives the
solution 4n (n + 1). But what if you did not recognise the Triangle
Numbers?*

$\sum_{k = 1}^{n}{\ \ \ (2k + 1)^{2} - 1}$ *= 4n (n + 1) ............
(1)*

*The pattern WILL continue 'forever' and can be shown through the Method
of Mathematical Induction, where we verify that the Statement is true
for n = k = 1; and then if the Statement is true for ANY n = k, it would
be true for the next*

*(where n = k + 1), then it will be true for ALL values of n.*

*You will find the method of proof by Mathematical Induction in TOPIC

**STOCKS AND SHARES - USING FIBONACCI LEVELS TOPIC 98**

When business enterprises launch, they raise capital by selling Shares
where the buyer awaits a dividend or he might decide to sell his Shares
later at a profit.

Wall Street (New York) established in 1792, and the London Stock
Exchange as early as 1801. It is claimed that the average return in all
that time was 10% p. a.

There are 2 main strategies used in the Trading in Shares. Warren Buffet
(born 1930, and worth over \$100 billion) uses Fundamental Analysis
where he looks for those companies strong in management structure that
are currently underpriced. The alternate method is Technical Analysis
that brings in the Golden Ratio as it assumes that markets are
influenced by 'humans' and human behavior follows patterns identifiable
to the Fibonacci ratios of 0%; 23,8%; 38,2%; 61,8%; 76,2%; 100% with 50%
thrown in. At these levels one can reach the 'levels of replacement'.

The amateur investor should learn more as it is generally recognized
that the general public react too late - at level 5 - when a movement is

A market on the ascendancy might be over-bought, so it undergoes
resistance, and tends to pull back (in one of those ratios) to a
position of support. The investor "Looks Left" to see if the new lower
level is in line with a level in the more recent past. Similarly, in a
falling market, there is an instinct to turn and move to a position of
Support -- again one "Looks Left" on the graph to compare to previous
lows.

The vital issue is "When to Buy?" or "When to Sell?" Some people keep
their shares for a long period, but many play the market to exploit the
short-term. Thus when shares graphs are shown, one sees stuttering
lines, called 'candles' -- red if negative and green if positive, whose
height indicates the highs and the lows of that period -- be it hourly
or daily or monthly or even over a decade or a century.

The graphic on the left (see the next page) shows a Retracement when a

at the bottom. When G drops below E, ascendancy stops. The graphic on
the right indicates when a market is falling (P to U). There might be a
resistance to over-selling, so it will turn positive. But if it turns

lower (T) than the previous high (R), it remains a downward trend. Some
SELL as the trend changes hoping that it hit its highest point before
dropping again. Sell at R or at T. But how do they know when these highs
and lows will occur?

It was Elliott who spotted or brought in the Golden Ratio as in the
Fibonacci series. The technical analyst would draw a horizontal line
through a chosen high point calling it 0 (or 100); and one through a low
point calling it 100 (or 0). To scale, the Fibonacci ratios are inserted
as lines. For the amateur, the final analysis comes too late as how can
one know exactly when a market changes -- that's the key.

On a rising market, one tries to see if the pull-back -- the replacement
-- pulls back to just short of one of these ratios -- like 38,2 or 61,8.
Then with a bit of Fundamental knowledge, they anticipate the turn. It
seems to be the case that "Fibonacci" works, but there are many ways of
being deluded. "Look Left". "Look Left" to check previous highs and
previous lows -- previous replacements and previous supports. The eyes
are always on those Fibonacci ratios -- and on 50%.
