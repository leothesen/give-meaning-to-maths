**MATHEMATICAL INDUCTION TOPIC 130**

Elsewhere, we saw 3 ways of PROVING that a 'FACT' is TRUE.

We can use the **DEDUCTIVE** method of employing the laws of LOGIC,
where we start with a KNOWN (the first PREMISE), use the approved laws
of logic and then accept the logical conclusion as a new fact going
forward. "If .... Then ...."

Examples of deductive thinking are employed in Euclidean Geometry:

If a = b If a = b If a = b

and a = c and c = d Then a \* c = b \* c

Then b = c Then a \* c = b \* d

( \* means any same operation to each side)

The Philosopher Plato, when asked how to create a Perfect World
(Utopia), said that all men get corrupted by Money (filthy lucre) and by
Nepotism (favouring your own relations), and said that only the
Philosopher King would be non-corrupt as well as all children under the
age of 10. So all others were to be killed. Then give the child a single
inalienable truth and the Laws of Logic and the world would be perfect.
This is the idea of Utopia. It sounds like a joke but Platonic
Utopianism is and will be behind the worst and most tragic murders of
other groups in the history of man.

A Judge in court uses the method of deduction. He has to be presented
with the starting point and then, with NO LOGICAL LEAPS, he hears the
presentation of the argument. He must see every vital "If ... Then ..."
with no jumps.

We (like a detective) can use the **INDUCTIVE** Method, where we start
at the CONCLUSION and work backwards to see if we can think backwards to
the First PREMISE - to the initial tenet. If that is determined, he then
lays the charge. But to convince the judge, he must present the argument
DEDUCTIVELY.

A great way of thinking is **REDUCTIO AD ABSURDUM** that is often used
by lawyers in court. They take the CONCLUSION and deny it. They will
suppose that X is NOT the murderer. So he takes the DENIAL of the Fact,
and uses the laws of logic. If he arrives at an ABSURD Conclusion, then
he has to question his first premise, and that was the non-fact. Hence
the 1^st^ Premise is correct.. his suspect IS guilty.

'A perfect woman must be XYZ. Kate is XYZ, hence Kate is a perfect
woman' is a deductive thought. I want Kate, hence what characteristics
has she got, and are those the features that I like. This is inductive
thinking.

'I want Kate. Pretend I do not have her. What is the consequence?' This?
That? I cannot live without her. I cannot "NOT have Kate". This is
Reductio Ad Absurdum.

Think of when you try to determine if a certain person is the love of
your life, or if a certain political party is for you, or if a certain
church is for you, or if you should follow a certain study career.

You can think Deductively from what you know and argue towards a
conclusion.

You can hold the Conclusion as a desirable, and work backwards to test
its veracity. Or you can deny the conclusion, and take its logical
consequences.

**MATHEMATICAL INDUCTION** is mainly used to test the
truth of those patterns that we asked "DO THESE PATTERNS GO ON FOREVER?"

**THE DOMINO EFFECT**

Think of a set of dominoes laid out in a line, on their thin edge,
reasonably close to each other so that if one falls, then the next
domino will fall. But we can only say that they ALL fall if (i) the
First one falls and (ii) then if any falls the next one falls. But that
first case one must be true, and then if any other case is true, the
next will be true, then ALL cases will be true.

A politician will try to get the first person in a street, and then tell
him to persuade his neighbour. If he reports back that he has the first
person, and he is sure that if he got anyone, he would get the next door
neighbour, then he would have ALL voting for him. This is the power of
advertising.

**\*Suppose we wanted to prove that the sum of successive odd numbers
produces a Perfect Square.**

|----------|------------------------|-----|:---:|-----|:---------:|
| Line 2 : | 1 + 3                  | =   |  4  | =   | $$2^{2}$$ |

Will the pattern continue FOREVER?

$\sum_{\mathbf{k = 1}}^{\mathbf{n}}{\mathbf{\ (2}\mathbf{k - 1}}$**) =**
$\mathbf{n}^{\mathbf{2}}$

**"The Sum of all ODD number from 1 to n is equal to a perfect
square".**

Step 1 : Consider Contention if n = k = 1

Left side = 2(1) -- 1 = 1 is equal to Right Side = $1^{2}$ = 1

So contention is true for n = 1 (the first case)

Step 2: Suppose the Contention is true for any n = k

Contention is 1 + 3 + 5 +7 + ... + (2k-1) = $k^{2}$

Then if n = k + 1 {add the next term to each side}

Contention is now

1 + 3 + 5 +7 + ... + (2k-1) + **\[2(k+1)-1\] =**
$k^{2} + $**\[2(k+1)-1\]**

= $\ \ k^{2}\  + 2k\  + 2 - 1$

= $k^{2}\  + \ 2k\  + 1$

= ( k + 1$)^{2}$

But n = k + 1, thus k = n -- 1.

Hence Sum = (n - 1 + 1$)^{2}$ = $n^{2}$

**Hence contention is true for n = 1**

**and being true for any n= k, it IS TRUE for the next (k + 1),**

**it is thus TRUE FOR ALL VALUES OF n.**

**[THE NATURAL or NAPIERIAN BASE** 'e' **TOPIC
132**

On your calculator press 'log 100'. The calculator takes it that the
base is 10. $\log_{10}100$ = log 100 = 2 because $10^{2} = 100.$

On your calculator press 'ln 100' which is $\log_{e}\ 100$ = 4,605170186

(read as: 'the Natural logarithm' - the Napierian log - the log if the
base is 'e')

Thus $e^{4,605170185}$ = 100.

At school you will use log 100 that is $\log_{10}100.$

At University, you will use 'ln 100' = $\log_{e}100.$ So what makes 'e'
so important?

Others actually developed the idea of logarithms to aid calculating, but
they let Scottish Mathematician John Napier take the credit. He referred
to Henry Briggs who made lists of logarithms to the accuracy of 14
decimal places.

When pupils in South African Schools, prior to 1983, used 'the log
book', accuracy was to 4 places -- and that was to the base of 10.

After all 100 = $10^{2}$ = $e^{4,605170186}$.

Which implies that
$\log_{10}\ 100\  = \ \ 2\ \ \ \ and\ \ \ \ \log_{e}100$ = ln 100 =
4,605170186.

Some books us the word 'log' when the base is 10 and 'ln' if the base is

Yet, many technical books will use the word 'log', though the base would

Read the Introduction of the book first. You would not want the bridge

Despite seeming to be a threat y = $e^{x}$ is the ONLY function in all
of Mathematics -- as University graduates will find out when they study
Calculus -- whose derivative is itself, whose rate of change is itself.
This is a phenomenal concept!

Our Matriculants will know that if y = $x^{2}$ - 2x - 3 is a given
function that can be written as f(x) = $x^{2}$ - 2x - 3 then its
gradient at any point (its rate of change at any point) called the
'derivative' will be $f'\ (x) = \ 2x - 2,$ that is a different
function.

Don't stress. The graph y = $x^{2}$ - 2x - 3 is a parabole that, as x
increases, y decreases till the turning point, where its gradient is
zero, and thereafter it increases. y = 2x -- 2 is the 'derivative' .
Where 2x -- 2 = 0, we have x = 1. And f(1) = -4. So that graph turns at
(1 ; -4).

Whatever, whatever, whatever function is given, its derivative (its rate
of change) is different to the given function - except for y = $e^{x}$
whose derivative is $f'(x) = \ e^{x}$ .

The only graph in the history of man, whose rate of change is itself at
any particular given point is y = $e^{x}$ .

This is powerful. Mind-blowing. It has made machined accounting
possible. It made computing possible. You will see this in time. It is
the very heart of machine calculating. What is happening 'on the
outside' is what is happening in the very heart of the computer doing
the calculator.
