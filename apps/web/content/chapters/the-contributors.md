**THE CONTRIBUTORS About the Graphic Artists**

I have relied on a couple of really BUSY and talented people for
Graphics. Besides the 521 'pictures', there are over 400 graphics and
over 500 photographs -- close to 1000 'pictures'. Luke Bell (LKB) is a
Computer Scientist, graduating at U.C.T. in 2015, now operating in
London. He added to Sections 12 to 17. Luke is a precise thinker and
this emerges.

Leo Thesen (LCT) with his friend Jordan Magrobi (Social Sciences) and
Steyn de Klerk (SDK) contributed good graphics in Section 17. They, with
Luke, were in my Mathematics class of Grade 11 in 2011 -- the last year
of my formal teaching. Leo qualified in Electrical and Mechanical
Engineering at U.C.T. and Steyn majors in Computer Science and
Information Technology at University of Stellenbosch.

In 2026, after the book was red-edited, Leo took the initiative in
'going digital' -- to the benefit of any high school pupil and / or
their teachers.

Doing the graphics is no easy task -- with some needing to be re-done.
But that one can know and find big-minded youngsters is a blessing not
to be lightly regarded.

Special Thanks to another Past Pupil Kieran Hunt (2010) for strategizing
the concluding 13 pages. Kieran mastered in Computer at Rhodes
University.

See end of Topic 83 and end of Topic 148 Solutions for recognition of
Jacob Lund's Investigations and Caleb Bell's graphics. The contribution
of Kendra Underwood, aged 22, designed the Cover page and the leading
pages.

**IMIZAMO YETHU**

A **Highlight** of my life was my being requested by
Headmaster Mr Derek Petersen to conduct Extra Lessons in Mathematics to
the Matriculation class of Imizamo Yethu Secondary School in George in
2011 and in 2012.Illuminating and Inspiring indeed as the group took the
leap of faith and decided to GIVE MEANING TO MATHEMATICS.They were taken
on an Excursion and lunch to Knysna. Not only did they groom
immaculately but they sang joyously all the way home.Friends in Knysna
donated money so that the entire class could attend their own
Matriculation Farewell. On another occasion, Knysna friends donated
Easter Eggs. Ex-Headmaster of York, Ron Dugmore and his wife Gill
Dugmore attended a lesson where a Canadian benefactor brought along a 12
year old Pearl of Great Price with a beautiful way of thinking.

Perhaps the **Biggest Influence** on me was a friend Derek
Denniss, a Cabaret artist, who -- on the saddest days in his life --
would still prepare for a gig for 2 hours, going over all the gestures
before a mirror. Nothing was left to 'The Holy Spirit'. He practised
every quip and gesture to perfection. Preparation, preparation,
preparation.

He regarded an evening as a success if patrons said that they really
enjoyed themselves -- not if they said that he was brilliant.

A teacher should ALWAYS prepare and then know that he / she has
succeeded if the class can leave excited -- having Mathematised --
having seen the Meaning in Maths. A teacher could well be a Cabaret
entertainer! Why not be a Cabaret artist in disguise?

Derek is seen here with Maggie and myself way back in 1975.
