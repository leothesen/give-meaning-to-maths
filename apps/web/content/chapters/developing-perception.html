SOLUTIONS FIFTEEN MATCHES TOPIC 04

If you take the 14th match, you will win.

Alert players very soon realise that anyone faced with 5 matches left cannot win. They take 1, you take 3. They take 2, you take 2. They take 3 and you take one, leaving them to take the 15th.

This means that if you take the 10th, then you should win. Most players realise this quite early, and it gives the clue to work in fours (14 – 10 = 4)

By the same thinking 14 - 10 = 10 – x. That ‘gap’ is 4.

So go for the 6th match. 6 – 4 = 2. So get that 2nd match out, and if you cannot,

then get the 6th one out, that enables you to get the 10th, and also the 14th.

You might, of course, bluff your opponent and become inventive.

* In the 1960s there was a big movement of Latin Americans into London. Their children struggled at school. The Education Department set up an Investigation into the Methods used to teach Mathematics.

The Chairman, a Professor, went to the streets and asked the Immigrant people: “What is

4 x 3?” The response was “We learnt it at school, but I have forgotten”.

He asked how much a particular lady paid to get to work. “A ride has just been increased to 87 pence.”

That particular week had a public holiday. He asked how much it would cost her to and fro work for the 4-day week. She blurted out: “6 pounds and 96 pence”.

He realised that Mathematics should be presented in meaningful situations that people could relate to. It gave momentum to “Situational Mathematics”.
