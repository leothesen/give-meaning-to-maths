STOCKS AND SHARES - USING FIBONACCI LEVELS TOPIC 98

When business enterprises launch, they raise capital by selling Shares where the buyer awaits a dividend or he might decide to sell his Shares later at a profit.

Wall Street (New York) established in 1792, and the London Stock Exchange as early as 1801. It is claimed that the average return in all that time was 10% p. a.

There are 2 main strategies used in the Trading in Shares. Warren Buffet (born 1930, and worth over $100 billion) uses Fundamental Analysis where he looks for those companies strong in management structure that are currently underpriced. The alternate method is Technical Analysis that brings in the Golden Ratio as it assumes that markets are influenced by ‘humans’ and human behavior follows patterns identifiable to the Fibonacci ratios of 0%; 23,8%; 38,2%; 61,8%; 76,2%; 100% with 50% thrown in. At these levels one can reach the ‘levels of replacement’.

In 1938, Ralph Newton Elliott proposed the Elliott Waves (see next page) showing positive ‘motives’ and negative ‘corrections’, generally in 5 steps. Steps 1,3 and 5 show the trend and steps 2 and 4 show the corrections.

Image 214

The amateur investor should learn more as it is generally recognized that the general public react too late - at level 5 - when a movement is all but complete.

A market on the ascendancy might be over-bought, so it undergoes resistance, and tends to pull back (in one of those ratios) to a position of support. The investor “Looks Left” to see if the new lower level is in line with a level in the more recent past. Similarly, in a falling market, there is an instinct to turn and move to a position of Support – again one “Looks Left” on the graph to compare to previous lows.

The vital issue is “When to Buy?” or “When to Sell?” Some people keep their shares for a long period, but many play the market to exploit the short-term. Thus when shares graphs are shown, one sees stuttering lines, called ‘candles’ – red if negative and green if positive, whose height indicates the highs and the lows of that period – be it hourly or daily or monthly or even over a decade or a century.

The graphic on the left (see the next page) shows a Retracement when a rising (A to F) market drops (at B, D, F). If the new low point (E) is higher than the previous low (C), the trend is still positive. It shows consolidation. Some people BUY as it drops at C or at E, hoping to buy at the bottom. When G drops below E, ascendancy stops. The graphic on the right indicates when a market is falling (P to U). There might be a resistance to over-selling, so it will turn positive. But if it turns

lower (T) than the previous high (R), it remains a downward trend. Some SELL as the trend changes hoping that it hit its highest point before dropping again. Sell at R or at T. But how do they know when these highs and lows will occur?

It was Elliott who spotted or brought in the Golden Ratio as in the Fibonacci series. The technical analyst would draw a horizontal line through a chosen high point calling it 0 (or 100); and one through a low point calling it 100 (or 0). To scale, the Fibonacci ratios are inserted as lines. For the amateur, the final analysis comes too late as how can one know exactly when a market changes – that’s the key.

On a rising market, one tries to see if the pull-back – the replacement – pulls back to just short of one of these ratios – like 38,2 or 61,8. Then with a bit of Fundamental knowledge, they anticipate the turn. It seems to be the case that “Fibonacci” works, but there are many ways of being deluded. “Look Left”. “Look Left” to check previous highs and previous lows – previous replacements and previous supports. The eyes are always on those Fibonacci ratios – and on 50%.

The Wall Street collapse of 1929 ruined many. It was caused by over-speculation and followed a period of huge progress in the United States that had applied Electro-Magnetism to produce machines, motors, ‘planes that led to prosperity.

Consequent to Germany’s defeat in World War I, the Peace Treaty of Versailles compelled them to pay huge compensation – and yet took the coalmines from Germany. Eventually, Germany simply printed money with no back-up. Workers took their pay home in wheelbarrows. A slump was inevitable.

In 2008, housing agents in the U.S.A. sold property to people not earning a salary and the market dropped. The super-rich are always able to monopolize, many others are uncertain. How honest are the Bulls and the Bears?

The simple soul wants to invest so as to maintain his level of well-being or just to allow it to increase. Greed seduces. There is so much implied in ‘Having Confidence in the Market’.
