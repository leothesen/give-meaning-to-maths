**THE MISSING DATES of 1752 TOPIC 22**

In the year 1752, in order to overcome discrepancies in 'official
dates', 13 days were simply wiped out in the system used at the time.
Why?

Julius Caesar in 46 BC replaced the AUC (Anno Urbis Conditre) that
commemorated the foundation of Rome as year zero. Our AD 1 (the start of
our Calendar about the time of the birth of Christ) was actually 753
AUC. Going on movements of the moon as well, the Year had 365 days, and
often days were ignored or added on, but these arrangements of date
tampering were secret, and open to bribery.

In a perfect world, a month should be 30 days, and the Earth would
revolve around the Sun in 360 days, making 12 months in a year.

But the earth rotates on an axis at an angle of 23$\frac{1}{2}°$
and revolves around the sun in a curve closer to an ellipse than a
circle.

Caesar had the year as 365,25 days, and Gregory had it as 365,2425 days,
and even Gregory's model will be a day out every 3030 years.

***SOME ASTRONOMIC CALCULATIONS TOPIC 24***

1.  How long does it take for the Moon to revolve around the
    Earth?

*The general answer is 1 month, being 30 days -- corrected to 29,53
days.*

*The sidereal time (fixed to the stars, not to the sun) is 27,32 days.
You can get the idea of the irregularities if you are a surfer and you
study the times of high tide on successive days. High Time is NOT at the
same time each day!*

2.  How long does it take for the earth to rotate once on its
    axis?

*The general answer is 24 hours. The sidereal day would be 23 hours 56
minutes and 4 seconds -- and even then there is a margin for
adjustment.*

3.  What is the time taken for the Earth to revolve around the
    Sun. The year has 365,24 solar days, being 366,24
    siderial ( a further fixed star) days in a year. *The standard
    answer used to be 360 days going from Equinox to Equinox. It is
    closer to 365 days 48 min 46 sec from Equinox to Equinox = 365,24219
    days compared a sidereal year of 365,25636 days..*

4.  What is the distance of the Sun to the Earth?

*The standard answer is 1,5 x* $10^{11}$ *metre or 1,5 x* $10^{8}$ *km,
corrected to*

*149 597 870 , 7 km, (about "150 million km") but even here there is an
allowed error, as the path is more elliptical, and solar flares reach
out quite a distance. Google claims 151, 64 million km. Why the

5.  What is the distance of the Moon to the Earth?

3*84 400 km -- again that path of the moon is elliptical, so this value

6.  What is the Equatorial Radius of the Earth?

6356 km - but the Polar radius is 6378 km. Surely you would ask "Why

A mere 100 years ago, Astronomers thought that ALL the stars were in The

in 26 000 years' time. The James Webb Telescope tracks down these
'earliest' masses to try to catch 'the moment of creation', operating
1,5 million km from earth -- compared to the Hubble at 570 km.
