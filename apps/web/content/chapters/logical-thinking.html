HOW CAN WE SAY “I KNOW” ? TOPIC 110

How do we Know that “This is True” & / or “That is False”.

Level 1 : Multiplying “Other Peoples’ Opinion”

If we think of our schooling, it seems that what we hold is TRUE are the statements made in text books or - in the Junior classes - what we can call ‘OTHER PEOPLE’S OPINION’ (OPO). We learn these off by heart so as to get ticked as correct. This sounds cynical, but – after all - we ought to strive not to be mere ‘book-learners’.

Sure those ‘Other People’ could be experts and could be well-meaning. They could be ignorant, or even malicious. We see that opinion is often multiplied, and so we yield, we bow to the Multitude of Opinion. We learn a load of these ‘True-isms’.

Is ‘P’ a Truth just because a lot of people said so? It is in ‘the book’. It ‘must’ be!

We could live in a wealthy democratic peaceful world, but the fellow who lives in a hostile poor enslaved environment might have different ideas on what is ‘true’. We say “THIS is true because Many People say so”. But it depends on whom the ‘Many’ are. This would be the lowest level of knowledge, called ‘Multiplying Opinion’.

Level 2 : Multiplying Evidence – the Scientific Method.

Above the Multiplying of Opinion is the Multiplication of Evidence – the SCIENTIFIC base. Here we see experiments or situations repeated. We accept what has appealed to our senses. We saw, heard, smelt, or we felt and so we accepted.

Science though is not that simple. Sure it calls for the evidence of experiments. But many times, the scientist HYPOTHESISES a Fact. He proposes the theory and then does experiments to prove himself ‘true’. Rather, he proposes what is less incorrect, and then challenges his Hypothesis to see if it can be even ‘less criticisable’. If he needs to adjust his Hypothesis, he will. Otherwise he would be a dogmatist.

But it can backfire. Centuries ago, illness was theorised as ‘the spirit of an evil force’. The remedy was to get a Shaman or Holy Person to bless the patient. But later, illness was seen as Bad Blood, and thus a ‘barber’ would cut and bleed the patient. Thereafter Illness was seen as Pain, and pain was alleviated by dosing the patient Opium was used. The cure was worse than the condition. In 1967, Dr Chris Barnard performed the first Heart Transplant because the ‘illness’ was having a defectively functioning organ – in this case, the heart was merely ‘a pump’ – and replaceable.

Science never claims its conclusions as dogmatically ‘true’, but allows for criticism. It will claim the least criticisable opinion as a ‘scientific’ fact. Few people realise this.

We do not ‘believe’ scientific facts. We hold them – until further evidence allows us to refine the theory. Science is not a case of ‘belief’, but of hypothesis – proposing.

Level 3 : The Aesthetic Sense. – the FEELINGS

There is another way whereby we Know – and that is seen in the world of Art, of Aesthetics. It could be music, painting, wine, architecture. Who decides? We do tend to accept the expert but every now and then, the opposites are seen as brilliant. Sometimes the ‘expert’ has a vested interest – only too often. The work of Picasso could look like a child’s doodling. Beethoven’s music was described as ‘Noise masquerading as Music’ – as was the Beatles. It goes down to harmony, finesse, balance – repeat Harmony, Finesse, Balance – the heart of the Aesthetic.

Level 4 : Logic Reasoning – the Mathematical Method – THE THINKING

Higher than these 3 sources of Knowledge (Other Peoples’ Opinion, The Evidence of Experiments and Aesthetics) is what we call the Mathematical Method – LOGIC – Reasoning. This is the high point of man’s ‘knowing’. Rational thinking at its best.

Summarised: By any or all of these means we can claim or deny whatever:

“P” is Rational Thinking (Logic) “P” is Not

“P” is Aesthetic Feeling “P” is Not

“P“ is Scientific Evidence “P” is Not

“P” is Other Peoples’ Opinion “P” is Not

ESTABLISHING THE LAWS OF LOGIC TOPIC 112

We will first write down the 4 laws of Logic, and then verify them.

  • In any argument we separate the FORM of the ARGUMENT from the CONTENT of the argument.

The Laws of Logic only govern the FORM of the argument – ‘STRUCTURE’.

  • In Conjunctions (using the word ‘And’) and in Injunctions (using the word ‘Or’) there are 2 Premises. 2 given facts (p and q ) that each be True (written as a ‘1’) or False (written as a ‘0’), from which we conclude and thus discover a new fact – a new truth. We will see this below.

  • In the Denial, there is One premise. We talk of ‘p’ or of ‘- p’, being (not p).

  • In the Implication, the first premise is the given ‘p’. The Consequent premise is the ‘q’ - the Conclusion of the Argument. “If p then q”.

  • A premise can be True (1) or False (2) in content. It is for someone else to determine Content as True or False. The LOGICIAN only looks at the FORM of the argument – not the Content - using the 4 Laws of Logic

Blaise Pascal was a very religious man at a time in Europe where people differed radically on issues of faith. He was once praised for his deep faith, and he replied: “Nobody could have greater faith than the poor Breton wife.” From Brittany on the coast of France. On another occasion, he wrote:

“The more I know, the more I know that I do not know.”

He set up what he considered as a tautology, an argument to validate a call to faith. It is called Pascal’s Wager.

It is criticised by both ‘believers’ and ‘non-believers’. It remains – however - a great example of reasoning. In time other causes were adopted to this form, and argued similarly – like Climate Change. We will test the Validity of the argument – the Form of the Argument. It is not a Proof of the Existence of God in itself. It cannot be. That would imply God owing his existence to a clever man, thus being less than the clever man - and hence not God. It is more of an Advertiser’s dream to use this argument, to persuade you that you cannot do without the ‘Coke” or whatever.
