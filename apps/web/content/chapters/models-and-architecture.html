Gaudi of Barcelona

Antoni Gaudi was born in 1852, and spent his spare time as a boy studying the structure of dessert-like plants that was to later be expressed in his style with an almost absence of straight lines. He only just passed at the University and it was said that he would not succeed. He went through a period of religious rebellion.

In 1882 the architect del Villa was chosen to build a unique cathedral devoted to The Holy Family (Jesus, Mary and Joseph) – the Sacrada Familia. St Joseph is the patron saint of workers, and thus the patron saint of Barcelona whose people had no love of the Spanish rulers in Madrid, having lost their independence in 1505.

A year later del Villa resigned and 31-year old Gaudi took over for the next 44 years. He devoted himself fully to the cause and his religious spirit deepened. Returning home one day, he was run over by a bus and died. Because he looked like a tramp, he was given no attention and was being prepared to be buried as a hobo. You need to see Gaudi’s work – even on the Internet to be totally fascinated.
