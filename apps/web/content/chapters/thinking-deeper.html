MATHEMATICAL INDUCTION TOPIC 130

Elsewhere, we saw 3 ways of PROVING that a ‘FACT’ is TRUE.

We can use the DEDUCTIVE method of employing the laws of LOGIC, where we start with a KNOWN (the first PREMISE), use the approved laws of logic and then accept the logical conclusion as a new fact going forward. “If …. Then ….”

Examples of deductive thinking are employed in Euclidean Geometry:

If a = b If a = b If a = b

and a = c and c = d Then a * c = b * c

Then b = c Then a * c = b * d

( * means any same operation to each side)

The Philosopher Plato, when asked how to create a Perfect World (Utopia), said that all men get corrupted by Money (filthy lucre) and by Nepotism (favouring your own relations), and said that only the Philosopher King would be non-corrupt as well as all children under the age of 10. So all others were to be killed. Then give the child a single inalienable truth and the Laws of Logic and the world would be perfect. This is the idea of Utopia. It sounds like a joke but Platonic Utopianism is and will be behind the worst and most tragic murders of other groups in the history of man.

A Judge in court uses the method of deduction. He has to be presented with the starting point and then, with NO LOGICAL LEAPS, he hears the presentation of the argument. He must see every vital “If … Then …” with no jumps.

We (like a detective) can use the INDUCTIVE Method, where we start at the CONCLUSION and work backwards to see if we can think backwards to the First PREMISE - to the initial tenet. If that is determined, he then lays the charge. But to convince the judge, he must present the argument DEDUCTIVELY.

A great way of thinking is REDUCTIO AD ABSURDUM that is often used by lawyers in court. They take the CONCLUSION and deny it. They will suppose that X is NOT the murderer. So he takes the DENIAL of the Fact, and uses the laws of logic. If he arrives at an ABSURD Conclusion, then he has to question his first premise, and that was the non-fact. Hence the 1st Premise is correct.. his suspect IS guilty.

‘A perfect woman must be XYZ. Kate is XYZ, hence Kate is a perfect woman’ is a deductive thought. I want Kate, hence what characteristics has she got, and are those the features that I like. This is inductive thinking.

‘I want Kate. Pretend I do not have her. What is the consequence?’ This? That? I cannot live without her. I cannot “NOT have Kate”. This is Reductio Ad Absurdum.

Think of when you try to determine if a certain person is the love of your life, or if a certain political party is for you, or if a certain church is for you, or if you should follow a certain study career.

You can think Deductively from what you know and argue towards a conclusion.

You can hold the Conclusion as a desirable, and work backwards to test its veracity. Or you can deny the conclusion, and take its logical consequences.

MATHEMATICAL INDUCTION is mainly used to test the truth of those patterns that we asked “DO THESE PATTERNS GO ON FOREVER?”
