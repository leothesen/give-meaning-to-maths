IMIZAMO YETHU

A Highlight of my life was my being requested by Headmaster Mr Derek Petersen to conduct Extra Lessons in Mathematics to the Matriculation class of Imizamo Yethu Secondary School in George in 2011 and in 2012.Illuminating and Inspiring indeed as the group took the leap of faith and decided to GIVE MEANING TO MATHEMATICS.They were taken on an Excursion and lunch to Knysna. Not only did they groom immaculately but they sang joyously all the way home.Friends in Knysna donated money so that the entire class could attend their own Matriculation Farewell. On another occasion, Knysna friends donated Easter Eggs. Ex-Headmaster of York, Ron Dugmore and his wife Gill Dugmore attended a lesson where a Canadian benefactor brought along a 12 year old Pearl of Great Price with a beautiful way of thinking.

Matriculants of Imizamo Yethu on an Excursion to Knysna 2011

Ntokosa Rwapuluza was to matriculate in 2018, and study at Nelson Mandela University, helped by the Jamers.

Perhaps the Biggest Influence on me was a friend Derek Denniss, a Cabaret artist, who – on the saddest days in his life – would still prepare for a gig for 2 hours, going over all the gestures before a mirror. Nothing was left to ‘The Holy Spirit’. He practised every quip and gesture to perfection. Preparation, preparation, preparation.

He regarded an evening as a success if patrons said that they really enjoyed themselves – not if they said that he was brilliant.

A teacher should ALWAYS prepare and then know that he / she has succeeded if the class can leave excited – having Mathematised – having seen the Meaning in Maths. A teacher could well be a Cabaret entertainer! Why not be a Cabaret artist in disguise?

Derek is seen here with Maggie and myself way back in 1975.
