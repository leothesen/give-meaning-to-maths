**TOMORROW & TOMORROW IS UPON US TOPIC 174**

Occasionally in the story of Mankind, great minds emerge and/or merge.
Leonardo da Vinci had brilliant ideas that did not turn to his own
commercial success. In earlier days, Curiosity and Inventions pervaded
as mankind evolved, but it is usually a crisis that brings out the
creative inventive best -- as in having to win a war.

Think of the technologies that have developed over the last 2 centuries.
Munitions, Rockets, Aircraft, Searching Space, Virus Threats, Artificial
Intelligence, Disease Control, Bio-Technology, Water Purification or
Water Pollution or simply Drought. Each era has its own crisis that
becomes a keg in its own further contribution to the Phenomenon of Man.

Entrepreneurs emerge like Elon Musk, and others of his ilk, who look to
alternate habitats, alternate energies, alternate transportation,
alternate food, alternate medicines, alternate behaviors, alternate
sources of energy. The Investor -- cum -- Entrepreneur -cum Exploiter
needs the Thinker and the Inventor. The clamour to get involved could
initially be based on Ethical, Humane or Religious principles, as Noble
Objectives are advocated. Man, however, can be Perverse, and can
identify with Limited or even Perverse Behaviour, and create a fear of
the 'Tomorrow'. Judging by the amount of court action in the AI world,
clashes are multiplying. In one touch of mockery, Musk referred to
Microsoft as Macro-Hard. The main clash is Musk versus Sam Altman C.E.O.
of OpenAI, with Musk suing him for \$135 million for reneging on an
assurance of free AI.

- ***I am using Capital Letters above to indicate the "flags" -- the
  Attributes - the Concepts, that occupy the minds of those dictating or
  leading the path to be tread.***

The drive of a web of entrepreneurs desirous of the money -- and the
fame -- or the pure power - that could be accumulated go for speed of
achieving the Objective - supposing that who gets there first, wins --
so speed enters the formula and becomes inordinate - overlooking
restraining corrections, in disloyalty, dishonesty and the myriad of
calamities that William Wordsworth would lament as "What Man Has Done To
Man."

So much has been happening as Entrepreneurs are seeking Energy -- being
Electricity that can hold Data -- and that means Chips. TSMV of Taiwan
is producing 60% of output globally and 90% of the more advanced chips.
They supply Apple, AMD and NVIDIA. Samsung concentrate on memory and
logic chips, with Nvidia leading the GPU market, with its chips made as
silicon wafers. Nvidia has had rough times yet the average increase over
the last 10 years has been 74%

Critics suggest that the errors in the answers to many early questions -
made to AI - is caused by the GPU chip, geared not to follow pure logic,
but to anticipate 'the next word'. This GPU approach had been used in
Games and Animation where the next move was anticipated without too much
consequence, rather than being a logical consequence.

- In these early stages, both GPU and CPU each run all computations --
  except Quantum machines - and logical functioning. General Processing
  Units (GPU) contrast to Central Processing Units (CPU). CPU is
  sequential as in a logical argument, whereas GPU is parallel, almost
  simultaneous. This applies to non-Quantum machines, of course.

To eliminate the tendency to err, huge amounts of data are fed into
memory -- and for this, more energy is needed. Energy is viciously
expensive. It could be likened to an artist -- having a holistic image
where each stroke builds the Classical artist's vision compares to a
dab-dab approach of the Impressionist - that could work - but perhaps
not.

Diamond chips could soon replace silicon chips where heat is a factor,
and that means that Diamond Prairie would replace Silicon Valley.

Technology will be immensely improved -- one million times, it is
forecast. Crystals are another possibility. The silicon used is
mono-crystalline. Nvidia's Jensen Huang has -- for 80% of today's
world - mastered the silicon chip for General Purpose Use, as well as to
High-Performance Computing and earning a citation as one of the
Architects of Artificial Intelligence -- a Phenomenon where demand far
exceeds supply. Demand expected in 2027 is 1 trillion dollars!

**YOU CAN'T KEEP MUSK OUT OF IT -** The vision of Elon
Musk is still that Man could / should / would reach out to the wider
cosmos -- even if that be a galaxy light years away! Jupiter's icy moons
might have water. Mars - being nearer - has been reached, but put on a
temporary hold as a destination, by Musk. This is not just because of
methane that emerges at night-time. He has delayed his dreams of a
'house' on Mars in favour of an initiative on the Moon. Eugene Cernan
was the last man to walk on the moon -- off Apollo in December 1972 --
54 years ago. Artemis 2's crew of 4 went on a flyby in April 2026 --
with focus on the far side. This has aroused a huge reaction and
investment from a number of nations including India and China.

The moon could become a depot, a launch pad (having less gravity than
earth) for further exploration, especially because of the mineral that
can be mined -- far exceeding what earth can supply. It will be able to
supply Lithium and of Helium 3 and other rare metals that can be used to
launch the multitudes of satellites from the moon not just to protect
earth, but to explore outer space. Enormous telescopes can be built in
the dark cold holes on the far side of the moon, where the temperature
is -240$℃.$ This work is now being done by the James Webb telescope
operating 1,5 million km from earth. Robots - by the way - might do the
mining for those rare minerals. The hydrogen and oxygen needed for space
crafts will come from the ice in the many holes on the far side of the
surface. Perhaps robots could even be the astronauts.

Musk's SpaceX dominates the myriad of satellites already blanketing the
earth so as to provide global Internet. He will be sending a horde --
hopefully a million - of Artificial Intelligence Computer satellites --
as data centres -- orbiting at about 2000 km from earth so as to stay in
the day-night zone. The aim is 1 million compared to the modest 8000
Starlink satellites in 2019. Costs hover at 1,25 trillion dollars. Sam
Altman of OpenAI -- no friend of Musk - does not share Musk's
enthusiasm. Googol aspires. The University of Vienna has already sent a
Quantum Computer with Musk's help in 2025 and China is lining up to do
the same -- ahead of Quantum's breakthrough. An analysis of all data
will be done on earth. Those machines that 'wear out' fall back to earth
and are burnt out on entry thus reducing the potential clutter of
litter.

Trapping Solar and Lunar will lead to Gallactic Energy and Ocean Wave
energy if need be to replace polluting Fossil Fuel energy - and causing
so many wars on earth! There are dangers because of bursts from the Sun.
Focus will be biological issues as well, on recuperating more speedily
from illness, never mind developing 'Stay Young' drugs. Replacing our
skin and body parts by synthetic products. Unmanned air transportation.
Issues undreamed of are being tackled and all this began because the
temperament of one Mad Scientist who "Never Take No For an Answer" -
Nikola Tesla (1856 to 1943).

Musk's stimulation and inspirer was born in Croatia in 1871 to a woman
who was an inventor, and whose father was a priest in his church. Nikola
Tesla cut his teeth with Thomas Edison, first in Paris and then in the
United States -- only to find that Edison, the great inventor, was not
exactly the man of his words when it came to paying. Tesla lived for his
ideas, yet he died in poverty -- with his only friends being a few
pigeons.

Tesla had followed the brilliant tracks of Faraday and Maxwell. His
understanding of electro-magnetic induction was to favour Alternating
Current transmission rather than the cumbersome Direct Current pursued
by Edison, whose employ he left and turned rather to Westinghouse, after
having succeeded in directing energy hydro-electrically from the Niagara
Falls to New York. Alternating Current set up the standard kitchen we
know of today. He dreamt of energy being transmitted wirelessly -- as in
cell phones -- and freely available!

Time has shown that Guglielmo Marconi (1874 -- 1937) was probably not
the original brains behind what we call 'the wireless', even though he
was awarded a Nobel prize therefor.

Tesla had wanted energy to be transmitted free. Neon lights, X-Rays,
Remote control -- idea followed idea. The failed Wardencliffe Towers
project sponsored by J.P. Morgan could have led to what later became the
World-Wide Web.

Those who provided other ideas were not too pleased, and Tesla suffered
when his factory was burnt to the ground. So too was Edison's. Tesla
died penniless, but it was his relentlessness and his preparedness to
think of the impossible that has inspired Elon Musk and the modern
'disciples'. To honour the 'Mad Scientist', Musk has named his electric
car - that may or may not dominate - after Tesla. Britain has declared
the non-Gas date to be 2030.

Musk had mastered computing, engineering and business finance, whilst
developing and selling systems like PayPal. His fear of mankind being
wiped out, led to SpaceX, stretching the vision to Mars and beyond --
though -- as said - he seems to have delayed the dream of Mars, focusing
now on the Moon. Initiatives like this, like Solar City, like Tesla
Motors are costly.

Millions have been invested and lost, but the dream goes on. On
September 9^th^, 2020, Musk lost \$16,4 billion due to Stock Market
movements and to General Motors deciding to go electric as well. Close
the door, so come through the window. On another day, millions would be
made or lost as with Twitter that became "X".

Musk, born in 1971, has been divorced twice and has 10 children,
starting with twins and then triplets. His latest son -- born in 2020,
is named \"X Æ A-Xii\". 

At the start of 2020, Elon Musk's wealth was stated as \$25 billion that
by July was \$59,3b and later exceeded $250 billion (September 2022).
Dropped to 188 billion by November 2022 after buying Twitter for 44
Billion. Now (March 2026) he is worth circa \$852 billion. He is rare
among the super-wealthy in that he himself initiates what he wants to
succeed in.

He receives favorable response for his Tesla initiatives, and in his
Space adventures -- like building a city on the Moon. He wants to hold
the energy that earth needs in a myriad of satellites. There are other
things in mind. He wants man to become a 'multi-planet species'. He said
that, in his youth, he read Science Fiction, and now 54-year-old Musk
wants that to be Science Fact.

**THE PHILOSOPHICAL BACKGROUND TO TODAY. Topic 175**

We all know the names of financiers and entrepreneurs Warren Buffett,
George Soros, Jack Ma of Alibaba, Steve Jobs of Apple, Bill Gates of
MS-Dos, Jeff Bezos of Amazon that dominate periodicals that boast of
huge fortunes.

Almost all have set up Foundations that aim to sponsor and reward
individuals or institutes that fulfill modern linked Global objectives
or 'flags' like "Climate Change", "Population Control", or "A Better
Life For All," or "Moral Re-armament" - all have their adherents and
demands.

Behind the growing crew of those obsessed by money are the thinkers who
emerge, and who leave an impact whether well or badly received. They are
increasingly questioning the ethics pervading and whether there ought to
be control. They do not stagger Change. They proclaim: "We ARE in the
new Era".

They might seem to be Utopian thinkers who desire the betterment of man
on earth -- and - like the modern AI generation and media - just mouth
the sentiment to impress investors. Utopia is a dream of The Perfect
World, with a Dystopia imaging chaos. But not everyone calling for
"Utopia" can be trusted.

***"Utopia"- meaning 'No such place' - is a dream idea. The early
Greeks - notably Plato - pushed for perfect states that earned the
blessings of the Gods. The great intellect, Sir Thomas Moore (1478 -
1535) in 1516, mocked the regime of Henry VIII and ended up as a martyr.
Gulliver's Travels by Jonathan Swift (1667 -- 1745) parodied George I of
England. Karl Marx (1818 -- 1883) motivated his ideal Socialist model on
'a better world'. Leading architects, like Le Corbisier (1887 -- 1965)
made modern architecture ostensibly 'Utopian'.***

When George Soros (born in Hungary in 1930) moved to London, he studied
Philosophy under (later Sir) Karl Popper (1902 -- 1994) -- a man of
immense intellectual dimension. Popper's book "The Open Society and Its
Enemies" had a huge impact on Soros, who then moved to U.S.A. and
developed hedge funds that could increase the wealth of his investors
whether the market rose or fell. He formed "The Open Society Foundation"
to give money to those causes that he wished to espouse. Popper's Open
Society had the idea of buoyancy, not absolutism, in it -- and thus he
built an affinity in investing in volatile markets to help espouse his
anti-totalitarian stance.

Popper had known of the French philosopher, Teilhard de Chardin (1881 --
1955), author of "**The Phenomenon of Man"-** who expounded a model of
successive evolutions leading from the paleontological, the biological,
through consciousness and on to spiritual unity, through to the
perfection of man as a machine -- a human being -- to reaching
perfection as a thinker and to the eventual spiritual evolution and
perfection -- as individuals and as a species. The ideas were Utopian,
as these philosophers realized or dreamed that the world simply would
become a 'Better Place', because it needed to, because the prevalent
tools were failing.

He was not in favour of the Utopian ruling of Man -- as in
Totalitarianism. He spoke of the arrival point -- the "Omega Point of
Divine Unity." Science and Religion were to meet each other. De Chardin
drew the framework -- as an Ultra-Futurist. Popper related it to the
world of his day seeking a way of facing the new reality of man deciding
his own future -- as an ideal. And the consequence of all of this in
giving man the ability to challenge and to take risks, allowed Quantum
Theory to enter the Story of Man; seeking new problems and new
possibilities.

Popper had proposed different models of a functioning new World model
\-- with the Scientific (Materialist) model gaining the mind of those
developing the technology we see now. He proposed that the good
scientist (technologist) would make **risky predictions** that could be
proven false, or could lead somewhere. Any postulation is criticizable,
and any conclusion would create a 'less criticisable', rather than a
'more perfect' tenet. In that way, being 'less criticisable' it was
'better world' scenario. Is this not the very thinking style of the
Quantum scientist. Think.

This is a far cry from the Modern Man heralding entrepreneurs whose
vision is to seek fame and excessive fortune independent of the
philosophical impact or even the materialistic impact seen by the number
of homeless, hopeless and unemployed left on the sidelines -- as we see
even in our country. Thought should transcend -- should sublimate,

**FUTURISTIC THINKING TOPIC 176**

Historically, our world always 'needs' to change, and could well change
by the influence of those who are now young, and by the
multi-billionaires who would want to capitalize on Artificial
Intelligence and Bio-Technology. A misfortune is that the emerging
entrepreneurs are 'preaching' that unless a person operates in
Artificial Intelligence, he /she will fall away because the 'machine' or
'robot' will be more skilled in processing, and -- imminently -- in
thinking. Thus the human would be reduced to being 'useless' -- or
redundant, at least.

Those children who might sit in a class right now where Exercise 1 is
followed by Exercise 2 by Exercise 3 by Exercise 4 and then a memory
test is given with special Prizes - until the syllabus is covered, will
struggle to handle - never mind to create - tomorrow. As futurist Gerd
Leonhard say: "If you work like a robot, the robot will overtake you".
How much of "Education" will be -- in time -- seen as robotic and
repetitive, dependent only on memory?

Merely "Memorising" does not lead to the thinking one needs for
tomorrow's world -- unless by the 'followers' and by the 'victims' --
the new unemployed. Somebody needs to invent tomorrow. Education will
need to change -- other than making mere piecemeal adjustments -- as
done with Covid-19, or a similar-pressured threat -- like the
'Artificial Intelligence" Saga

Somebody must identify the mis-directions of today. Merely knowing
Mathematics will no longer effectively help. Thinking Mathematically
will be a big boost -- and being curious, inquisitive and imaginative
would allow you to be the human factor who can exploit the machines and
the robots and sublimate being human. Bravely taking a risk on a great
idea is a high point. Remember, machines cannot MOTIVATE the -- where a
Government Department's report proved to be as unintelligible as it was
erroneous, though it might have been costly to assemble.

*"Money Maketh Not The Man"* but these super-rich could sponsor the
Thoughts of Those Who Can and Do Think Deeper, or they could search for
talent - even in poor areas. It is an Inter-subjective world that we
live in. The Super Rich have turned -- almost en-masse -- to the
development of Artificial Intelligence that 'changes everything'.

Those seeking fame and fortune openly predict job losses and a destroyed
and demotivated society, but they forget that not all men are
materialists. Spiritualism is deep in the East and in India.
Christianity's objective is universality. Leaders of thought, with other
objectives, and with better morality, will arise and challenge the
apathy and even the despair of the generation. Accepting Divinity will
not just be a matter of convenience but a spiritual conviction. That
final Evolution of Teilhard de Chardin in "The Phenomenon of Man" could
well emerge. Even the leading Philosopher of the 20^th^ Century,
Jean-Pierre Sartre (1905 -- 1980) in "Being and Nothingness" focused on
Giving Meaning to Existence. Would man just capitulate to the
distraction of 'Artificial Intelligence'?

twice and made a few decisions that upset his seniors.

Like his own father, he was awarded the Medal of Honour.

Somehow Kipling's "If" is more humane and less militaristic than that of
MacArthur.

If you can keep your head when all about you

Are losing theirs, and blaming it on you

If you can trust yourself when all men doubt you,

But make allowance for their doubting too;

If you can wait and not be tired of waiting.

Or being lied about, don't deal in lies.

Or being hated, don't give way to hating.

And yet don't look too good, nor talk too wise.

**If** you can dream and not make dreams your master;

If you can think, and not make Thoughts your aim;

If you can meet with Triumph and Disaster

And treat those two Imposters just the same;

If you can bear to hear the truth you've spoken

Twisted by knaves, to make a trap for fools;

Or watch the things you gave your life to, Broken

And stoop and build them up with worn out hands.

**If** you can make a heap of all your winnings,

And risk it all on one turn of pitch-and-toss,

And lose, And start again at your beginnings

And never breathe a word about your loss.

If you can force your heart and nerve and sinew

To serve your turn long after they have gone,

And so hold on when there is nothing in you

Except the Will that says to them "Hold on".

**If** you can talk with crowds and keep your virtue.

Or walk with Kings nor lose the common touch.

If neither foes nor loving friends can hurt you:

If all men count with you; but none too much;

If you can fill the unforgiving minute

With sixty seconds worth of distance run.

Yours is the earth -- and everything in it,

And what is more -- you'll be a Man, my Son
