Question: The Chinese would put any similar shapes on the 3 sides of a right angle triangle, and then pack the shapes with RICE grains. What did they conclude?

The Chinese covered the AREA of similar shapes, on each side of the right angled triangle with rice grains, just like we can place little squares from graph paper. The fact that the area covered on the hypotenuse was equal to the sum of that covered by similar figures on the 2 other sides, demonstrated the theorem in a practical way.

Chinese records show what is called the Gou Gu – what we call Pythagoras’ Theorem - that evolved from their skill in land surveying and controlling floods. This goes back to 2000 B.C. when it was used to assess the distance to the sun, using the shadow of the midday sun. The Greek Eratosthenes held the same theory at the same time!
