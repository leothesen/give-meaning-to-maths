Many many many hours later, I communicated with Jacob Lund whom I had

Did I say 'cannot'?

Euler ( 1707 -- 1783) developed this insight from a dot (called a Point
or a VERTEX, from which he drew a line - called an EDGE. Euler realised
that this 'dot ... line' is the very same origin as in Euclid's
Geometry. The first elements were the DOT - and the dot moves to become
a LINE. Euler did the same, calling the 'dot' a **'VERTEX'** and the
'line' **AN 'EDGE'**, and any 'region' that might form, a '**FACE**.'

The question of how many Regions (Faces) form if regular or irregular
polygons have their diagonals drawn may have amused minds long before
Euler, but he analysed the thinking and gave his name to a character
'**chi'** that linked the number of Regions (called FACES)
signified as F, to the number of Dots or Nodes (called VERTICES (plural
of Vertex), signified as V, to the number of Lines (called EDGES) and
signified as E. He was to seek and to deduce the link between F, V and
E.

By deductive thinking, he came out with the formula that

F + V -- E = the Euler character; or "Regions + Nodes -- Border lines".

It would be great if we could strategise, and think it out ourselves,
without reverting to EULER. He devised a formula relating the 3 elements
in the exercise -- a dot (Vertex or Node), a line (Edge); an area (Face
or Region).

How great if WE could do so. It was not my intention to steal a solution
