**HOW CAN WE SAY "I KNOW" ? TOPIC 110**

*How do we Know that "This is True" & / or "That is False".*

**Level 1 : Multiplying "Other Peoples' Opinion"**

If we think of our schooling, it seems that what we hold is TRUE are the
statements made in text books or - in the Junior classes - what we can
call 'OTHER PEOPLE'S OPINION' (OPO). We learn these off by heart so as
to get ticked as correct. This sounds cynical, but -- after all - we
ought to strive not to be mere 'book-learners'.

Sure those 'Other People' could be experts and could be well-meaning.
They could be ignorant, or even malicious. We see that opinion is often
multiplied, and so we yield, we bow to the **Multitude of
Opinion**. We learn a load of these 'True-isms'.

Is 'P' a Truth just because a lot of people said so? It is in 'the
book'. It 'must' be!

We could live in a wealthy democratic peaceful world, but the fellow who
lives in a hostile poor enslaved environment might have different ideas
on what is 'true'. We say "THIS is true because Many People say so". But
it depends on whom the 'Many' are. This would be the lowest level of
knowledge, called 'Multiplying Opinion'.

**Level 2 : Multiplying Evidence -- the Scientific
Method.**

Above the Multiplying of Opinion is the **Multiplication of
Evidence** -- the SCIENTIFIC base. Here we see
experiments or situations repeated. We accept what has appealed to our
senses. We saw, heard, smelt, or we felt and so we accepted.

Science though is not that simple. Sure it calls for the evidence of
experiments. But many times, the scientist HYPOTHESISES a Fact. He
proposes the theory and then does experiments to prove himself 'true'.
Rather, he proposes what is less incorrect, and then challenges his
Hypothesis to see if it can be even 'less criticisable'. If he needs to
adjust his Hypothesis, he will. Otherwise he would be a dogmatist.

But it can backfire. Centuries ago, illness was theorised as 'the spirit
of an evil force'. The remedy was to get a Shaman or Holy Person to
bless the patient. But later, illness was seen as Bad Blood, and thus a
'barber' would cut and bleed the patient. Thereafter Illness was seen as
Pain, and pain was alleviated by dosing the patient Opium was used. The
cure was worse than the condition. In 1967, Dr Chris Barnard performed
the first Heart Transplant because the 'illness' was having a
defectively functioning organ -- in this case, the heart was merely 'a
pump' -- and replaceable.

Science never claims its conclusions as dogmatically 'true', but allows
for criticism. It will claim the least criticisable opinion as a
'scientific' fact. Few people realise this.

We do not 'believe' scientific facts. We hold them -- until further
evidence allows us to refine the theory. Science is not a case of
'belief', but of hypothesis -- proposing.

**Level 3 : The Aesthetic Sense. -- the FEELINGS**

There is another way whereby we Know -- and that is seen in the world of
Art, of Aesthetics. It could be music, painting, wine, architecture. Who
decides? We do tend to accept the expert but every now and then, the
opposites are seen as brilliant. Sometimes the 'expert' has a vested
interest -- only too often. The work of Picasso could look like a
child's doodling. Beethoven's music was described as 'Noise masquerading
as Music' -- as was the Beatles. It goes down to harmony, finesse,
balance -- repeat Harmony, Finesse, Balance -- the heart of the
Aesthetic.

**Level 4 : Logic Reasoning -- the Mathematical Method -- THE
THINKING**

Higher than these 3 sources of Knowledge (Other Peoples' Opinion, The
Evidence of Experiments and Aesthetics) is what we call the Mathematical
Method -- LOGIC -- Reasoning. This is the high point of man's 'knowing'.
Rational thinking at its best.

**Summarised**: By any or all of these means we can claim or deny
whatever:

"P" is Rational Thinking (Logic) "P" is Not

"P" is Aesthetic Feeling "P" is Not

"P" is Scientific Evidence "P" is Not

"P" is Other Peoples' Opinion "P" is Not

**The "God" factor**

Consider the Question of Religion -- the 'God' factor -- that we could
refer to as 'G'.

The existence of 'G' is not ultimately dependent on any of the 4
platforms above. When we are young, we are indeed told -- so it comes
mainly as Other People Said and it was for us to trust the goodwill &
integrity of those people - often parents.

We ask Questions on Causality and can get scientific confirmation. There
can also be experiences one has that bring peace and feeling good. There
is even a tendency to ask questions and verify logically. No problem. To
some people the existence of a God simply does not come from any of the
above 4 constructs. The Existence of a God actually depends on a higher
platform: Faith -- which is simply 'Acceptance' -- in response to a gift
given to believe -- to accept. Faith supersedes knowledge. The existence
or the non-existence of 'G' is not in itself dependent on Other People's
Opinion. If the majority of people say 'G' is , then 'G' 's existence
would depend on the Opinion of Man, and thus can not be a God by the
very concept of 'Godness'. It is like Man creating God for convenience.

Similarly, if we concluded that 'G is' or 'G is not' by **Scientific
Evidence** then it is this evidence that is 'creating' the 'God'
that defeats the concept of a 'God'. This does not prevent major
religions about talking on the glories of creation, and the laws in the
fascinating Universe. It is valid and very necessary for man to enquire.

It is in the **World of Aesthetics** that many prefer to find
peace. Some people like to 'feel' the presence of a God and hence they
accept, because we like to feel good.

Three of the 5 women to be declared a Doctor of the Church were Teresa
of Avila, (1515 to 1582), Therese of Lisieux (1873 to 1897) and Mother
Teresa of Calcutta (1910 to 1997). They are each highly regarded for
their 'faith'. Each of them said that in her entire life, she never once
FELT that God existed. They felt the opposite -- all their lives. They
called it 'the dark night of the soul'. 'God' is not the product of
Feeling. Faith asks for Acceptance, whether or not there are good
feelings.

The highest level by which Rational Man can think and know is by using
LOGIC. If 'G is' is accepted because of **Logical
Reasoning** then 'G' would have been created by the thinking
man, and that defeats the concept of 'Godness'.

Those, whatever their culture, who do say "There is a God" do so not by
evidence, feeling or logic, but by Faith, which is the simple Acceptance
of what cannot be heard, evidenced, felt or reasoned. Faith is a
response of Acceptance. The Acceptance of what might defy Opinion,
Experiment, Feeling and/or Logic.

Non-Faith is Non-Acceptance*. "I Believe" means "I Accept" as a strong
decision.*

**ESTABLISHING THE LAWS OF LOGIC TOPIC 112**

We will first write down the 4 laws of Logic, and then verify them.

- In any argument we separate the **FORM** of the ARGUMENT from the
  **CONTENT** of the argument.

The Laws of Logic only govern the **FORM** of the argument --
'STRUCTURE'.

- In Conjunctions (using the word 'And') and in Injunctions (using the
  word 'Or') there are 2 Premises. 2 given facts (p and q ) that each be
  True (written as a '1') or False (written as a '0'), from which we
  conclude and thus discover a new fact -- a new truth. We will see this
  below.

- In the Denial, there is One premise. We talk of 'p' or of '- p', being
  (not p).

- In the Implication, the first premise is the given 'p'. The Consequent
  premise is the 'q' - the Conclusion of the Argument. "If p then q".

- A premise can be True (1) or False (2) in content. It is for someone
  else to determine Content as True or False. The LOGICIAN only looks at
  the FORM of the argument -- not the Content - using the 4 Laws of
  Logic
